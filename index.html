Abstract

Adapting vision-and-language navigation (VLN) agents to continually evolving environments is essential for dynamic real-world deployment. However, naively fine-tuning agents on new scenes often leads to catastrophic forgetting of previously learned scenes and degraded generalization to unseen environments. In this paper, we propose EverWalker, a lifelong VLN agent designed to thrive in an ever-changing world by evolving its navigation knowledge through a prototype-driven journey. Instead of storing independent step-specific adapters, EverWalker introduces a Dynamic Prototype Bank (DyproBank) that incrementally accumulates compact scene prototypes, capturing essential navigation knowledge. Building upon this evolving prototype space, we further propose a Prototype-conditioned Hypernetwork (PHYP-Net) that dynamically generates task-adaptive LoRA parameters conditioned on aggregated prototypes, enabling efficient step-specific inference without explicit task identifiers. We also introduce a multi-level distillation strategy to facilitate progressive knowledge accumulation across continual scenes. Extensive simulator and real-world experiments are performed to demonstrate the effectiveness and superiority of EverWalker.

Method

Illustration for the proposed EverWalker. Our framework consists of two core components: A Dynamic Prototype Bank maintains an evolving library of scene prototypes with soft routing for knowledge aggregation. A Prototype Hypernetwork dynamically generates task-specific LoRA conditioned on aggregated prototypes, enabling adaptive navigation while preserving shared knowledge.

Task Setting

Illustration of our Lifelong Vision-Language Navigation (LVLN) benchmark. The benchmark consists of a sequence of 24 distinct navigation tasks. The blue region (Tasks 1--18) represents the Continual Learning Phase, where the agent sequentially learns across diverse seen environments. The orange region (Tasks 19--24) denotes the Zero-Shot Generalization Phase, evaluated on entirely unseen scenes to assess the agent's evolving adaptability.

Real-World Experiments Setting

Illustration of the proposed robotic navigation system. The platform integrates a quadruped robot for mobility, a camera for visual perception, and a remote server for running the EverWalker agent, enabling closed-loop real-world navigation. To validate the effectiveness of EverWalker in real-world scenarios, we construct a physical robotic navigation platform as illustrated in Figure. The hardware configuration consists of a quadruped robot (DeepRobotDog Lite2) serving as the mobile agent, a high-definition RGB camera (Hikvision DS-E12) mounted for egocentric perception, a high-bandwidth portable WiFi communication module, and a remote computation server equipped with 8 NVIDIA RTX A6000 GPUs.
